1763-1789

Room 2: Colonial Times / A New Era

This unit follows the colonies as they turned protest into revolution and then tried to build a new nation. The Revolution made liberty central to American identity, but the founding left major questions unresolved.

Artifact Declaration Table, 1776

Historical Context

The American Revolution challenged British authority and argued that governments should depend on the consent of the governed. The Declaration of Independence made the phrase "all men are created equal" one of the most powerful statements in American history.

Connection to the Claim

The Revolution expanded ideas of freedom by rejecting monarchy, defending natural rights, and creating a republic. At the same time, many founders owned enslaved people, women received no political rights, and Native nations often lost land and autonomy after the war. The promise grew, but its application remained limited.

Artifact Significance

John Trumbull's painting became a symbol of founding ideals. Its significance comes from the contrast between the universal language of equality and the narrow group of people who actually received full citizenship and political power in the new nation.

Key Evidence

  • After 1763, British taxes and imperial policies intensified colonial resistance.
  • The Declaration of Independence announced equality as a national ideal.
  • The Revolutionary War lasted from 1775 to 1783.
  • The Constitution and Bill of Rights created a stronger republic while leaving many people outside full citizenship.
